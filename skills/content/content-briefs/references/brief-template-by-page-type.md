# Brief Template by Page Type

The general brief structure from the main SKILL.md, adapted for the most common page types. Each adapted template emphasizes the sections most important for that page type and trims sections that matter less.

## Pillar Page Brief

Pillar pages anchor a topic cluster. They are comprehensive, long-form, and serve as the canonical reference for the cluster's primary query.

Emphasis:

- **Strategic context:** the cluster role is critical. The writer needs to understand this is the hub.
- **Structural outline:** detailed. Pillar pages have many sections; each one needs clear purpose.
- **Internal links:** comprehensive list of every supporting page in the cluster (links go both directions).
- **Original elements:** pillars should include unique data, named frameworks, or distinctive perspective that supporting pages reference.

De-emphasis:

- Standard schema is usually Article; nothing exotic needed.

Length: 2,500-5,000 words. Brief itself: 1,500-2,000 words.

## Supporting Article Brief

A piece that lives within a cluster, addresses a specific subtopic, and links to the pillar.

Emphasis:

- **Pillar context:** the writer needs to see the pillar page (in draft or published form) to understand how this piece fits.
- **Internal links:** to the pillar (always) and to 2-4 sibling supporting pieces.
- **Target query and intent:** specific. Supporting pieces target narrower queries than pillars.

Length: 800-1,800 words. Brief: 500-800 words.

## Comparison Page Brief

"[Product A] vs [Product B]" structure. Heavy on factual specificity.

Emphasis:

- **Target query:** "[A] vs [B]" exactly, including any modifiers ("for small teams," "for enterprises").
- **Format requirements:** comparison tables, clear verdicts per use case, side-by-side feature views.
- **Top 5 currently ranking:** comparison SERPs have strong format expectations; the writer needs to see them.
- **Original elements:** real testing or real user data per product is the difference between a comparison page that ranks and one that does not.
- **Off-limits sources:** competitor blogs that have written their own comparison; the brand should not link to or cite these.

Length: 1,500-2,500 words. Brief: 700-900 words.

## Listicle Brief

"[N] Best [Things] for [Use Case]" structure. Easy to do badly; harder to do well.

Emphasis:

- **Inclusion criteria:** how items get on the list. Without criteria, the listicle reads as arbitrary. ("Selected for [X], [Y], and [Z] criteria.")
- **Order rationale:** ranked, alphabetical, by use case? The order needs a reason.
- **Per-item depth:** brief specifies the content depth per item (a paragraph each, a section each, etc.).
- **Authoritative sources:** listicles that cite no sources read as one person's opinion. Cite where claims come from.

Length: 1,500-3,000 words. Brief: 600-1,000 words.

## FAQ Page Brief

A page or section composed of questions and answers.

Emphasis:

- **Question sources:** Where do the questions come from? PAA, customer support tickets, sales calls, Reddit threads. Listed explicitly with examples.
- **Answer length:** typically 50-100 words per question. Long enough to fully answer, short enough to be extractable.
- **Schema:** FAQPage schema is critical.
- **No padding:** FAQ that pads to look long fails extractability. Each question gets its proper answer length, no more.

Length: variable; depends on question count. Brief: 400-700 words.

## How-To Brief

Procedural content. Step-by-step instructions for a specific outcome.

Emphasis:

- **Outcome statement:** what the reader will accomplish, stated specifically at the top.
- **Prerequisites:** what the reader needs to have or know before starting.
- **Step structure:** numbered, each step actionable, each step verified by someone who has actually done the thing.
- **Common errors and fixes:** what goes wrong and how to fix it. Lifts the page above other how-to content.
- **Schema:** HowTo schema (note that Google has reduced visible rich results for HowTo but the schema still helps AI engines parse the page).

Length: 1,000-3,000 words depending on complexity. Brief: 500-800 words.

## Case Study Brief

A specific customer or project story, structured for SEO and credibility.

Emphasis:

- **Source material:** named customer, real outcomes, real data. The brief specifies what permissions are in place.
- **Structure:** typically Situation → Challenge → Solution → Outcome, with real numbers throughout.
- **Quotes:** at least two direct quotes from the named customer.
- **Target query:** often "[Service/Product] case study," "[Customer] [outcome]," or industry-specific terms.
- **Voice:** more journalistic, less promotional, even when promoting outcomes.

Length: 800-1,800 words. Brief: 500-700 words.

## Location Page Brief

A page targeting a specific geography for a local service business.

Emphasis:

- **Per-location proof points:** the brief lists the specific local details the writer must include (landmarks, school districts, HOA names, named neighborhoods served, recent projects with photos).
- **Differentiation requirement:** explicit statement that the page must differ meaningfully from other location pages. Templated content is the failure mode.
- **Schema:** LocalBusiness with the location's actual data (coordinates, service area, opening hours).
- **Internal links:** to the city-level pillar (if applicable), to adjacent neighborhood pages, to relevant service pages.

Length: 800-1,500 words. Brief: 500-700 words. See `programmatic-content` if building location pages at scale.

## Comparison Categories (e.g., "Best [Category]")

A specific listicle subtype targeting commercial-investigation queries.

Emphasis:

- **Methodology:** how the picks were chosen. Real methodology beats "we picked the best."
- **Use-case-specific picks:** different picks for different use cases within the category. "Best overall," "Best budget," "Best for small teams," "Best for enterprise."
- **Per-pick specificity:** real pros, real cons, real pricing, real screenshots.
- **Affiliate or partnership disclosures:** if applicable, disclose.

Length: 2,000-4,000 words. Brief: 800-1,200 words.

## Concept Explainer ("What Is X?")

A page defining and explaining a specific concept.

Emphasis:

- **Definition first:** the first sentence is the definition. Inverted pyramid, no preamble.
- **Plain language:** even for technical concepts, the explanation should be accessible.
- **Examples:** concrete examples beat abstract definitions for both extractability and reader comprehension.
- **Related concepts:** brief links to adjacent concepts in a glossary or cluster.

Length: 800-2,000 words. Brief: 400-700 words.

## Adapting Templates

Every brief should be tuned to the specific piece, the writer's experience level, the brand's complexity, and the editor's preferences. The templates above are starting points; copy and modify rather than copy and use verbatim.

A writer who has produced 20 pieces for the brand needs shorter briefs than a writer producing their first piece for the brand. An experienced writer in a topic area needs less SERP context than a new writer learning a new category. A regulated industry (legal, medical, financial) needs more constraint sections than a general consumer category.

The goal across all variations is the same: give the writer enough that they can produce a strong piece without coming back with questions; leave them enough freedom that the piece reads as written by a thinking human, not assembled to specification.
