# Content Strategy: Cardinal Ridge Roofing Co.

> Fictional example for illustration. Brand name, URL, and findings are made up.

**Date:** 2026-05-24
**Strategist:** Agent (using `content-strategy` skill, with client-context, keyword-research, content-audit, and competitor-analysis loaded)
**Strategy horizon:** 12 months (May 2026 to May 2027)
**Last reviewed:** 2026-05-24

## Decision 1: Primary Business Outcome

**Lead generation.** Specifically: increasing qualified estimate-request form submissions and tracked organic-source phone calls from 22 per month (baseline) to 50 per month within 9 months (per client-context goals).

Secondary outcome: building entity authority on insurance claim work to support the AEO program described in the AEO category strategy.

## Decision 2: Primary Audience

Homeowners aged 35-65 in the Greater Indianapolis metro (Marion, Hamilton, Hendricks, Johnson counties) who have a damaged or aging roof. About 60% are storm-event driven (hail, wind damage with a recent storm trigger); 40% are planned-replacement (roof is 15+ years old, planning the work proactively). What they care about, in order: (1) whether the contractor will handle the insurance claim paperwork, (2) whether the crew shows up when promised, (3) whether the work will last.

Secondary audience: property managers handling small multi-family buildings (4-20 units) and a small number of HOAs.

## Decision 4: Cadence

- **Production cadence:** 5-6 substantive pieces per month. One writer working roughly half-time, owner SME input on ~30% of pieces. Owner review at 48-72 hour turnaround per client-context constraint.
- **Refresh cadence:** quarterly review of pillar pages (5 pages reviewed each quarter), biannual review of high-value supporting pages, annual review of tail.
- **Calendar:** Google Sheet (small program; database tool not yet warranted). Editorial planning weekly, full review monthly.

## Decision 5: Ownership and Measurement

| Role | Person | Cadence |
|---|---|---|
| Strategy owner | Cardinal Ridge owner | Quarterly strategy review |
| Editorial owner | Office manager (with agency support) | Weekly editorial planning, monthly review |
| Writers | One primary writer (agency); occasional guest pieces from owner | Per assignment |
| Approval flow | Writer → editor → owner review (48-72hr) → publish | Per piece |
| Distribution owner | Office manager | Per publish (social posts, email to opt-in list, GBP post) |

**Measurement:**

- Output (monthly): pieces published, pieces refreshed, cluster status
- Engagement (monthly): GSC impressions, clicks, average position per cluster
- Outcomes (quarterly): estimate-request form submissions from organic, tracked organic-source phone calls, cluster-level conversion attribution where possible
- Tools: Google Search Console, GA4 (with the AEO referral segments per AEO citation-tracking skill), call tracking software for organic-source phone tagging, the AEO tracking spreadsheet for citation-rate trending

Baseline (May 2026): 22 estimate-request submissions per month from organic. Target (Feb 2027): 50 per month.

## Off-Limits and Constraints

Per client-context:

- No content suggesting insurance fraud or aggressive claim tactics (insurance cluster gets reviewed for this on every piece).
- No content promoting specific roofing material brands in a way suggesting exclusivity (material comparisons present GAF and competitors evenhandedly).
- No major site redesign in the next six months (content fits the existing Divi theme).
- Owner reviews all pieces personally, 48-72 hour turnaround. Schedule pieces accordingly.

## Notes

- The strategy assumes the technical-audit P0 work (redirect chain cleanup) and AEO foundations (schema deployment, entity consistency) are completed in parallel. Content shipped to a technically broken site underperforms.
- The named insurance methodology (Cluster 2 piece) is the highest-leverage AEO investment per the AEO category audit. Treat as a flagship piece, not a routine resource page.
- The storm-damage original data piece is shared between this strategy, the off-page-link-building strategy, and the AEO citation-tracking baseline. Coordinate so the launch is a single coordinated event in October 2026.
- The owner's son is being groomed for digital-marketing involvement. Recommend bringing him into the quarterly strategy review starting Q3 to begin the handoff.
- Re-evaluate Cluster 5 (Maintenance) at the Q3 strategy review. If Clusters 1-4 are tracking ahead of plan, expand Cluster 5 commit; if behind, defer to Year 2.
