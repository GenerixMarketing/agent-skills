# Cluster Development Worksheet

A guided worksheet for developing each topic cluster in the content strategy. Walk through the questions per cluster candidate. The worksheet IS the deliverable for each cluster decision.

## Cluster Candidate: [Working Name]

### Stage 1: Concept Validation

**1. What is the cluster about, in one sentence?**

[Plain language. If you cannot describe it in one sentence, the cluster is not yet defined.]

**2. Why does this cluster matter to the primary audience?**

[Specific to the audience defined in the strategy. "Because it is useful" is not enough. What problem does the audience face that this cluster helps solve?]

**3. What is the brand's claim to expertise here?**

[Years of practice, proprietary data, named experts, customer outcomes, unique perspective. Defensibility requires something specific.]

**4. Which competitors already own this cluster, if any?**

[From competitor analysis. If a competitor is already deeply established in this space, decide: compete head-on, take an adjacent angle, or pick a different cluster.]

### Stage 2: Strategic Fit

**5. Which primary business outcome does this cluster serve?**

[Cross-reference the strategy's primary outcome. If the cluster does not serve it, document why it should be included anyway, or drop it.]

**6. Where in the funnel does this cluster live?**

- Top of funnel (awareness): the customer does not know they have a problem yet
- Mid funnel (consideration): the customer knows the problem and is exploring solutions
- Bottom of funnel (decision): the customer is evaluating specific options including the brand

[A cluster can span multiple funnel stages with different pages. Document which pages serve which stages.]

**7. How does this cluster connect to existing brand priorities?**

[New service launch, market expansion, product release, leadership change, competitive threat. Cluster work that ties to a real business priority gets resourced; orphan clusters get deprioritized.]

### Stage 3: Demand Verification

**8. What are the candidate primary queries for this cluster?**

[List 10-30 candidate queries. Pulled from keyword research, customer support, sales calls, PAA, Reddit, AI-search testing.]

**9. What is the combined estimated monthly search volume?**

[Sum of the candidate queries. From keyword research tools.]

**10. What is the average difficulty across primary queries?**

[KD score average from keyword research. Realistic threshold depends on site authority.]

**11. Are there shadow-demand signals beyond search volume?**

[Reddit threads with high engagement, AI Overview presence on related queries, support ticket frequency, conference session topics. Sometimes the highest-value content lives in spaces traditional keyword research underweights.]

### Stage 4: Cluster Structure

**12. What is the pillar page topic?**

[The central hub of the cluster. Comprehensive coverage of the topic at the level the audience needs. Usually the broadest, highest-value query in the cluster.]

**13. What are the supporting page topics (target 5-15)?**

[Each one a specific subtopic, comparison, FAQ, or worked example that serves the cluster theme. Each one its own page with its own target query.]

**14. How will internal linking work?**

[Pattern: every supporting page links to the pillar; pillar page links to every supporting page; related supporting pages cross-link where topically relevant. Document the anchor text patterns to ensure descriptive linking, not generic "click here.")

**15. What schema applies to each page type in the cluster?**

[Article schema on most pages; FAQPage on FAQ sections; LocalBusiness on local pages where relevant; cross-reference schema-for-aeo skill in AEO category.]

### Stage 5: Production Plan

**16. Who writes pieces in this cluster?**

[Specific named writers. If the cluster requires deep subject expertise, document who provides that expertise (SME interview, founder review, etc.).]

**17. What is the priority order for the cluster?**

[Pillar first or supporting pieces first? Most clusters build best with the pillar as either the first or last piece: first to anchor the linking structure, last to consolidate after supporting depth exists. Specific to the cluster.]

**18. What is the realistic timeline to ship the full cluster?**

[5-15 pieces at the brand's production cadence. A small program may take 4-6 months per cluster; a medium program 2-3 months; a large program 1 month.]

**19. What is the refresh cadence after ship?**

[Pillar pages refresh quarterly to twice a year. Supporting pages: as the underlying information changes; minimum annual review.]

### Stage 6: Measurement

**20. What does success look like at 3 months, 6 months, 12 months?**

- 3 months: indexation health, initial impressions, first rankings on long-tail queries
- 6 months: meaningful traffic to supporting pages, pillar page ranking on broader queries, some assisted conversions
- 12 months: cluster operating as a topical authority signal, pillar page ranking top 10 for primary query, measurable contribution to primary business outcome

[Adjust thresholds to the brand's authority and competitive context.]

**21. What is the kill criterion?**

[Under what conditions does the cluster get deprioritized? "If we cannot rank top 30 on any primary query by month 12, we revisit whether the cluster fits." Kill criteria force honest evaluation.]

## Worksheet Decision

Based on the answers above, the cluster is:

- [ ] **Approved.** Add to the strategy with the structure defined above.
- [ ] **Conditionally approved.** Approved pending one or more open questions (document which).
- [ ] **Deferred.** Real opportunity but not the right time. Document why and what would unlock it.
- [ ] **Rejected.** Document why and any insights from the analysis that inform other cluster choices.

## When to Re-Run This Worksheet

- For every new cluster candidate during strategy development.
- For every existing cluster at the quarterly strategy review (does it still fit, still defend, still produce results?).
- When a major event changes the underlying assumptions (new competitor, market shift, brand pivot).

A cluster that does not survive re-evaluation should be sunset gracefully: existing content refreshed and maintained, new content paused, the strategic attention reallocated.
