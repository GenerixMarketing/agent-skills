# GBP Optimization Checklist

Complete field-by-field checklist for a fully optimized Google Business Profile. Walk through every section. Most profiles are 30-60% complete; this brings them to 90%+.

## Section 1: Business Information

- [ ] **Business name.** Legal business name only. No keyword stuffing. No location modifier unless it is part of the legal name. ("Cardinal Ridge Roofing Co." not "Cardinal Ridge Roofing - Best Indianapolis Roofer.")
- [ ] **Primary category.** The most specific available match for the core service. Browse the full category list before settling on one; Google has tens of thousands of categories and the right specific one outperforms a generic parent category.
- [ ] **Secondary categories.** Up to 9. Add only legitimate additional services the business actually provides. Padding hurts.
- [ ] **Description.** Up to 750 characters. Lead with what the business does and where. No URLs, no promotional language ("best in town"), no special characters. Write for humans first, search second.
- [ ] **Opening date.** Set accurately. Long-established businesses gain trust from this field.
- [ ] **Address.** Either a public visitable address OR clearing the address and setting a service area (mutually exclusive). For service-area businesses, hide the address and define the service area as one or more cities, regions, or zip codes.
- [ ] **Service area.** For service-area businesses. List specific cities, regions, or zip codes served. Up to 20 areas.
- [ ] **Hours.** Accurate for every day of the week. Use "Open 24 hours" only if actually open 24/7.
- [ ] **Special hours.** Update for every holiday and unusual closure. Google emails reminders before major holidays; act on them.
- [ ] **Phone number.** Primary phone matching the rest of the citation profile (NAP consistency). If a tracking number is used elsewhere, the canonical phone should still match across GBP, website, and major directories.
- [ ] **Website URL.** Canonical homepage URL.
- [ ] **Appointment URL.** If applicable, link to a booking page (Calendly, vendor scheduling, internal booking system).
- [ ] **Menu URL.** Restaurants only.

## Section 2: Services

For service businesses, this section is one of the strongest relevance signals.

- [ ] **List every service offered.** Use Google's predefined service names where available; create custom ones for services without a predefined match.
- [ ] **Service description.** Up to 300 characters per service. Be specific: what is included, who it is for, what the customer can expect.
- [ ] **Service price.** Set "From" or "Fixed" prices where applicable. Optional but signals professionalism.
- [ ] **Service category groups.** Organize services into logical groups when the list is long. Improves user experience and clarity.

## Section 3: Products

For applicable businesses (retail, food, some services).

- [ ] **Product name.** Clear, descriptive.
- [ ] **Product photo.** Real photo, not stock.
- [ ] **Product description.** 150-300 characters; what it is, who it is for.
- [ ] **Product price.** Specific or starting-from.
- [ ] **Product category.** Group similar products.

## Section 4: Attributes

Check every accurate attribute. Google adds new ones regularly; audit quarterly.

Common service business attributes:

- [ ] Accessibility (wheelchair accessible, accessible entrance, accessible parking, etc.)
- [ ] Amenities (free Wi-Fi, parking lot, etc.)
- [ ] Crowd (LGBTQ+ friendly, transgender safe space, etc.)
- [ ] Health and safety (mask required, staff wear masks, sanitization between visits, etc., where applicable)
- [ ] Offerings (online estimates, free consultations, financing available, etc.)
- [ ] Payments (accepts cash, cards, mobile pay, checks)
- [ ] Planning (appointment required, walk-ins welcome)
- [ ] Service options (in-store shopping, in-store pickup, curbside pickup, online care, delivery, takeout, dine-in)
- [ ] Highlights (Veteran-owned, Women-owned, Family-owned, Black-owned, etc.)
- [ ] Recycling (battery, e-waste, etc., where applicable)

## Section 5: Photos and Videos

GBP weights recent photo uploads. Maintain a cadence rather than dumping all photos once.

- [ ] **Logo.** Square format, high resolution.
- [ ] **Cover photo.** Landscape, high resolution, represents the business at its best.
- [ ] **Interior photos.** 5+ for businesses customers visit. Show the actual space.
- [ ] **Exterior photos.** Storefront, signage, parking area.
- [ ] **Team photos.** Owner, staff, real people working.
- [ ] **Product or service photos.** Completed work, finished products, before-and-afters where applicable.
- [ ] **Action photos.** Team working, customers in the space (with permission), service in progress.
- [ ] **360° / Street View.** Consider commissioning a Google trusted photographer for an interior virtual tour.
- [ ] **Videos.** Up to 30 seconds each, up to 100MB. Show the team, the space, the process.

Cadence target: 5-10 new photos per month for an active SMB. Less for established profiles already photo-rich.

## Section 6: Posts

Posts appear in the GBP panel for ~7 days. Use them to signal activity and reinforce messaging.

- [ ] **Update posts.** General updates, news, announcements.
- [ ] **Offer posts.** Time-bound promotions with start and end dates.
- [ ] **Event posts.** With date, time, location.
- [ ] **Product posts.** Specific products with details.

Cadence target: weekly minimum. Each post: ~150-300 words of body, one photo, a clear CTA button (Learn more, Call, Book, Buy, Sign up).

## Section 7: Q&A

A section many businesses ignore. Free real estate for SEO and customer education.

- [ ] **Pre-seed common customer questions.** Submit and answer 5-10 of the most-asked questions. Anyone with a Google account can submit; submit from the business owner's account.
- [ ] **Monitor for new user-submitted questions daily.** Notifications are not always reliable; check actively.
- [ ] **Respond promptly and helpfully.** Each Q&A entry is visible to future searchers.
- [ ] **Upvote helpful answers.** Both your own and user-submitted ones that are accurate.

## Section 8: Reviews

The single biggest GBP ranking factor after categories.

- [ ] **Review velocity.** At least one new review per week for an active SMB. Steady cadence beats bursts.
- [ ] **Star average.** 4.5+ is healthy. Below 4.0 needs investigation and response.
- [ ] **Response rate.** 100% target. Respond to every review.
- [ ] **Response time.** Within 24-48 hours of posting.
- [ ] **Response quality.** Specific to the review, not templated. Thank for positive; address concerns for negative; never argue.
- [ ] **Review request system in place.** Automated or human-triggered request after every completed job or sale.

See `reviews-playbook.md` for detailed program design.

## Section 9: Messaging

If the business is set up to respond to messages quickly, enable.

- [ ] **Messaging on.**
- [ ] **Welcome message set.**
- [ ] **Response time target met.** Google publishes typical response time on the profile. Slow response times look worse than no messaging at all.

If messages cannot be responded to within an hour during business hours, leave messaging off.

## Section 10: Bookings (if applicable)

Some industries support direct booking integrations via partner platforms.

- [ ] **Booking partner connected** (Vagaro, Booksy, GlossGenius, Resy, OpenTable, Square Appointments, etc.).
- [ ] **Booking link verified.** Test booking flow end-to-end quarterly.

## Section 11: Insights Monitoring (Ongoing)

GBP Insights reports performance data. Pull monthly.

- [ ] **Total searches** broken into direct, discovery, branded.
- [ ] **Search queries that surfaced the business.** Useful for content and keyword work.
- [ ] **Customer actions:** website clicks, direction requests, phone calls, messages.
- [ ] **Photo views compared to similar businesses.**

Use the queries data to inform `keyword-research`. Use the actions data to assess ROI of GBP work.

## Section 12: Profile Health Audits (Ongoing)

- [ ] **Weekly:** check for new reviews to respond to, new Q&A to answer, suspicious edits suggested.
- [ ] **Monthly:** post cadence held, photo cadence held, all sections still accurate.
- [ ] **Quarterly:** category audit (Google adds and reorganizes), attribute audit, services list audit, full NAP consistency check.
- [ ] **Annually:** full profile completeness sweep against this checklist.

## Spam to Report

If competitors are gaming GBP, report via the Business Redressal Form (search "Google Business Profile redressal form"). Persistent reporting works.

Common spam to report:

- Business names stuffed with keywords ("Best Indianapolis Roofer Co.")
- Fake or duplicate listings
- Listings at addresses where the business does not actually operate
- Obviously fake reviews (bot patterns, generic praise, no detail)
- Listings categorized incorrectly to compete in a different category's local pack

Document the issue with screenshots. Submit. Follow up if no action within 4 weeks.
