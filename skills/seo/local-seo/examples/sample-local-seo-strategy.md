## Executive Summary

Cardinal Ridge has a foundationally healthy Google Business Profile that is underoptimized rather than broken, and an on-site local SEO presence that has obvious gaps (no LocalBusiness schema, thin neighborhood pages already flagged elsewhere). Recommended sequencing puts GBP completion in week 1, schema deployment in week 2, then parallel-tracks both for ongoing program work.

## Track A: GBP Optimization Plan

### Immediate (Week 1-2)

Complete the GBP audit gaps in this order:

- **Services itemization:** List all 12 services Cardinal Ridge actually offers (currently only 4 listed). Add description per service. Set "From" pricing where applicable.
- **Business description:** Rewrite to use the full 750-character budget. Lead with what they do (residential roof replacement and repair), the service area (Greater Indianapolis metro), and what differentiates them (family-owned since 2003, insurance claim expertise). No URLs, no superlatives.
- **Attributes:** Add all accurate attributes. Family-owned, Veteran-owned (if applicable), Online estimates, Free consultations, Financing available, Accepts insurance, Wheelchair accessible (if office visits are an option), Spanish spoken (if applicable).
- **Q&A:** Pre-seed 8 common questions submitted from the owner's account:
  1. How quickly can you get out for a roof inspection?
  2. Do you handle insurance claim paperwork?
  3. What materials do you install?
  4. What warranty do you offer?
  5. Do you offer financing?
  6. Do you serve [Carmel / Fishers / Westfield / Greenwood]?
  7. How long does a typical roof replacement take?
  8. Are you licensed and insured?
- **Photos:** Upload 15 new photos: 5 completed Indianapolis-area jobs, 3 team photos, 3 process photos (crew on a roof), 2 truck/equipment, 2 owner-portrait-style (humanizes the profile).

### Reviews Program

- **Target velocity:** 6-10 new reviews per month within 90 days of program launch.
- **Review request process:** SMS sent within 24 hours of every job completion. Sent from the dispatch software via a direct deep link to the Google review form. Sender is the project manager's name, not the company name.
- **Response standards:** All reviews responded to within 24 hours. Positive responses are specific and personal (mention the project, the crew, the location). Negative responses acknowledge, offer service recovery via direct phone, and never argue in public.
- **Owner:** Office manager owns the workflow setup and weekly monitoring; owner reviews and approves the first 20 responses to standardize tone.

### Posts and Photos Cadence

- **Posts:** weekly. Mix of completed-project highlights, seasonal tips, occasional special offers when relevant. Each post 200-300 words, one photo, clear CTA.
- **Photos:** 5-10 new per month, sourced from job-site photos already being taken for internal documentation.
- **Owner:** office manager publishes; owner approves once per month.

### Q&A Strategy

- **Pre-seeded questions:** see list above, published in week 1.
- **Monitoring cadence:** daily check by office manager. Notifications enabled but cannot be trusted as the only source.
- **Response standards:** every user-submitted question gets a helpful answer within 48 hours.

### Ongoing Hygiene

- **Suspicious edit monitoring:** weekly check by office manager. Any unexpected edit reverted immediately.
- **Holiday hours updates:** Indianapolis-relevant calendar maintained (Memorial Day, Independence Day, Labor Day, Thanksgiving Thursday and Friday, Christmas Eve and Day, New Year's Day). Updates published 30 days in advance.
- **Competitor GBP spam monitoring:** quarterly review. Specifically watch for: business names with keyword stuffing, fake review patterns, listings at fake addresses. Report via Business Redressal Form when found.

## Track B: On-Site Local SEO Plan

### Foundation (Week 1-2)

- **Deploy RoofingContractor JSON-LD schema** sitewide. Use the Service-Area Business template from `references/local-schema-templates.md`. Areas served: Indianapolis, Carmel, Fishers, Westfield, Greenwood, Noblesville, Zionsville, Brownsburg, Plainfield, Avon, Greenfield. Validate via Google's Rich Results Test before pushing live. Dev owns implementation.
- **Add BreadcrumbList schema** to every non-homepage URL. One template change.
- **Audit footer NAP** for microdata wrapping; not strictly necessary if LocalBusiness schema is sitewide but does not hurt.
- **Sitewide local signal review:** confirm city name appears in title tag and H1 of every location page; confirm at least one paragraph of city-specific content on each.

### Location Pages

- **Existing pages to rebuild:** the 12 thin neighborhood pages already in the content roadmap. Track B specific requirements per rebuild:
  - LocationSpecific schema per page (using the multi-location template adapted for service areas)
  - Embedded Google Map showing the service area for that neighborhood
  - Minimum 3 named local proof points per page (neighborhood landmarks, common roof types in the area, recent project addresses with photos where customer permission granted)
  - Cross-links to 3-5 nearby neighborhood pages
- **New pages to create:** 3 secondary neighborhood pages (Noblesville, Zionsville, Brownsburg) already in the keyword-research Tier 2 list. Same Track B requirements.
- **Avoid:** templating 50 zip-code pages to chase coverage. The Helpful Content System penalizes this. Five strong pages outperform fifty thin ones.

### Geo-Targeted Content

- **Cornerstone local guides to create:**
  - "Best time to replace a roof in Indiana" (already in keyword-research Tier 1)
  - "What Indianapolis homeowners should know about hail damage"
  - "Seasonal roof maintenance calendar for central Indiana"
- **Existing content to refresh with local angle:** the storm damage tips post and the Carmel home maintenance post both perform modestly and would benefit from a 2026 refresh with current data.
