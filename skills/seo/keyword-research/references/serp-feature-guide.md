# SERP Feature Guide

Each common SERP feature, what it means for clicks, and how to optimize.

The unifying point: ranking #1 in 2026 does not mean what it meant in 2016. The SERP features above and around the blue links absorb a meaningful share of attention. Plan accordingly.

## Featured Snippet (Position Zero)

**What it is.** A highlighted answer block at the top of the SERP, drawn from a single page. Usually a paragraph, list, or table.

**Click behavior.** When the snippet fully answers the query, click-through to the source drops sharply. When the snippet teases but does not complete the answer, click-through to the source is excellent, often higher than position #1.

**How to win it.**

- Answer the implied question in 40-60 words, directly under a clear heading.
- Match the format Google is currently using for the query (paragraph, ordered list, unordered list, table).
- Use the exact phrasing of the query in the answer or the heading above it.
- Build the rest of the page around supporting depth so the user clicks through for the full picture.

**Strategy.** Worth pursuing when the answer is teachable but the depth is large. Worth avoiding when the answer is fully resolvable in two sentences, since you give away the click.

## People Also Ask (PAA)

**What it is.** An expandable block of related questions, each with a short answer pulled from a different page. Clicking one expands more questions infinitely.

**Click behavior.** Each PAA expansion is a small CTR opportunity. The block also expands the SERP downward, pushing position #2 and below further from the user's eye.

**How to win it.**

- Use an FAQ section on the page with the exact PAA questions as H3 headings.
- Answer each in 30-50 words, then link to a deeper page if more depth exists.
- Add FAQPage schema to the section.
- Cover questions Google is already showing in the PAA box for related queries (this surfaces new question slots over time).

**Strategy.** Cumulatively meaningful. Many pages can capture two or three PAA slots, and each one is real impressions.

## AI Overview

**What it is.** Google's AI-generated summary at the top of some SERPs, with cited sources linked. Introduced in 2024, expanded through 2025.

**Click behavior.** Substantial click cannibalization. The user reads the AI Overview and often does not need to click. Cited sources see brand impressions but not always clicks.

**How to win it (be cited).**

- Write content that is factually specific (named entities, numbers, dates, places).
- Structure for extractability: clear headings, short paragraphs, definitions in the first sentence, lists where appropriate.
- Establish authority signals (author bios with credentials, citations, recent dates).
- Cover the exact question phrasing the user is likely to ask.
- Be the source on a topic, not the eleventh derivative article.

**Strategy.** For informational queries, AI Overviews are the new front line. AEO content frameworks beat traditional SEO content frameworks here. See the AEO category skills when shipped.

**When AI Overview presence kills a keyword target.** If the AI Overview fully answers the query for most users, and the query does not lead to a buying action, the ROI of ranking under the Overview is poor. Either pivot to a related transactional term or accept the impression-only outcome.

## Local Pack (Map Pack)

**What it is.** A map with three local business listings, shown for queries with local intent.

**Click behavior.** Dominant for local queries. The three local pack slots typically capture 40-60% of clicks on a local SERP. The blue links below the local pack get whatever is left.

**How to win it.**

- Optimize Google Business Profile (categories, services, hours, photos, posts, Q&A).
- Earn reviews consistently.
- Build local citations (NAP consistency across directories).
- Get backlinks from locally relevant sites.
- Ensure on-site signals: city in the page title, full address visible, LocalBusiness schema.

**Strategy.** For any service business with a physical or service-area presence, local pack ranking is more valuable than organic blue-link ranking for the same query. Invest in GBP first.

## Image Pack

**What it is.** A row of image thumbnails with a "View all" link, usually shown for visual queries ("kitchen designs," "roof shingle colors," "logo examples").

**Click behavior.** Captures clicks from queries where the user wants to see something. Less directly conversion-relevant for most businesses, but useful for visibility.

**How to win it.**

- Original photos (not stock).
- Descriptive file names and alt text.
- Compressed and properly sized.
- Hosted on the indexable page that should benefit from the click.

**Strategy.** Visual-heavy businesses (interiors, fashion, home services, food) should prioritize. Service businesses with minimal visual content can deprioritize.

## Video Carousel

**What it is.** A horizontal row of video thumbnails, usually YouTube-hosted.

**Click behavior.** Captures clicks from queries where the user wants to watch ("how to," "review," "demo").

**How to win it.**

- A YouTube presence is the easiest path (Google owns YouTube).
- Video schema on hosted videos on the client's site.
- Match the title and thumbnail to the query.

**Strategy.** Worth pursuing for how-to and demo queries. Pure SEO sites can ignore.

## Knowledge Panel

**What it is.** A box on the right side of the SERP, usually for branded queries, showing the business's name, photos, address, reviews, hours, social links.

**Click behavior.** Reduces the need for users to click into the site for basic info (address, hours, phone). But high brand-trust value, and a strong knowledge panel often correlates with high CTR.

**How to win it.**

- A complete and verified Google Business Profile.
- Wikipedia or Wikidata entries when applicable.
- Consistent business name, address, and phone across the web.
- Schema markup that confirms the entity.

**Strategy.** For local and service businesses, table-stakes. For ecommerce, less critical.

## Shopping Results

**What it is.** Product cards with prices, ratings, and a merchant link, shown for product queries.

**Click behavior.** Dominant for transactional product queries. Free organic shopping listings (since 2020) and paid Shopping ads compete here.

**How to win it.**

- Submit a product feed to Google Merchant Center.
- Product schema on every product page.
- Maintain pricing and availability accuracy.

**Strategy.** Ecommerce-only. Service businesses can skip.

## SERP Feature Audit on a Keyword

When auditing a candidate keyword's SERP, run this quick check:

1. Is there an AI Overview? (If yes, plan for citation-not-click.)
2. Is there a featured snippet? (If yes, consider whether you can win it.)
3. Is there a local pack? (If yes, GBP is your main lever.)
4. Is there a PAA box? (If yes, plan FAQ section.)
5. Are there video, image, or shopping features? (If yes, factor into content type.)
6. How many "real" blue link results are above the fold after all of the above?

For a query with AI Overview + featured snippet + PAA + local pack, the answer to #6 might be zero. That keyword's real opportunity is not blue-link #1, it is one of the SERP features. Plan accordingly.
