# Off-Page and Link Building

This skill produces an off-page authority strategy the user can execute. The output is a short, defensible list of tactics ranked by realistic outcome, with templates and tracking the user can take to a stakeholder or a freelancer.

The skill takes a strong posture: white-hat only. Most link-building content for sale online is grey-hat at best. Following it puts the client at risk of a manual action (Google penalty) and reputational damage. This skill refuses to recommend tactics in that category. See "What This Skill Will Not Do" below.

## When to Run This Skill

Run it when:

- The user asks how to build authority, get backlinks, or improve domain authority
- The user has done on-page and technical work and rankings still lag (off-page is often the bottleneck for competitive terms)
- The user is launching a digital PR push or hiring a PR freelancer and needs a strategy
- The competitor analysis surfaced a backlink gap as a major finding
- The user is asking about specific tactics (HARO, podcasts, partnerships, local citations) and wants to know which to prioritize

Skip it when:

- The user wants on-page optimization (use `on-page-audit`)
- The user wants technical fixes (use `technical-audit`)
- The user wants keyword research (use `keyword-research`)
- The site has fundamental on-page or technical issues unresolved (fix those first; links amplify what is already working, they do not save what is not)

## Before You Start

### Read client-context.md

If the file exists, read it. The industry, location, business stage, and existing brand relationships shape which tactics will work. A 20-year-old family-owned local roofer has very different link opportunities than a 2-year-old SaaS startup. The competitor list also tells you what backlink patterns already exist in the space (from `competitor-analysis`).

### Inventory the existing backlink profile

In order of preference:

1. **Ahrefs Site Explorer.** Best-in-class for backlink data. Pull referring domains, top pages by backlinks, anchor text breakdown, lost vs gained over time.
2. **Semrush Backlink Analytics.** Comparable; some practitioners prefer it. Use the MCP if available (`mcp__96c65232-927d-4ecd-8575-0da349b2fb3f__backlink_research`).
3. **Google Search Console Links report.** Free, less complete, but trustworthy on what Google actually sees.
4. **Free Ahrefs Backlink Checker.** Top 100 backlinks per domain. Enough for small-site triage.

For each pull, capture:

- Total referring domains
- Domain rating estimate
- Top 10-20 referring domains (who already links)
- Anchor text distribution (any spammy patterns?)
- Lost-link count over the last 6 months (any reclamation opportunities?)
- Top-linked pages on the site (where existing equity is concentrated)

### Set realistic expectations

Off-page SEO is the slowest of the three workstreams (on-page, technical, off-page) by a wide margin. A meaningful link campaign takes 6-12 months to show results. Set this expectation with the user before any tactical work begins. Quick-win promises in this area are usually a sign of grey-hat tactics.

## The Strategy (Five Workstreams)

Walk through these five. The order is roughly highest-leverage to lowest, but order varies by client.

### 1. Digital PR and Expert Sourcing

The most defensible link-building work in 2026. Earn editorial mentions from real publications by being a quotable expert source.

**Tactics:**

- **Expert sourcing platforms.** Connectively (formerly HARO), Qwoted, Featured, JournoFinder, Help A B2B Writer. Reporters post requests; sources respond with quotes. Successful pitches earn an editorial mention and (usually) a link.
- **Reactive PR.** When a relevant news event happens (storm season, regulation change, industry incident), pitch reporters with an expert take within 24 hours. Reporters are under deadline; speed wins.
- **Original data and surveys.** Publish a small original-data piece (e.g., "We surveyed 200 Indianapolis homeowners about their roof replacement experiences"). Pitch it to relevant publications. Earns coverage AND links because reporters cite the source.
- **Thought leadership op-eds.** Pitch trade publications with bylined opinion pieces from the founder or a senior expert.

**What to capture per opportunity:**

- Publication name and rough authority (DR / DA estimate)
- Topic relevance to the client's domain
- Realistic likelihood of placement
- Owner: who responds to pitches and writes the quote

**Realistic outcome:** A consistent expert-sourcing program produces 2-6 placements per month for an active source. Each placement is one editorial link from a real publication. Cumulatively meaningful.

### 2. Local Citations and Industry Associations

Foundational, especially for local businesses. Less glamorous than digital PR, often more impactful for local search visibility. Pairs with `local-seo`.

**Tactics:**

- **NAP consistency across the major citation sources.** Google Business Profile, Apple Maps, Bing Places, Yelp, Yellow Pages, BBB, Angi, HomeAdvisor, Foursquare, Facebook, plus any industry-specific directories.
- **Local press citations.** Get listed in local business directories run by the chamber of commerce, the BBB, local economic development organizations.
- **Industry association memberships.** Trade associations almost always have a member directory that links out to members. National Roofing Contractors Association, local builders associations, etc.
- **Sponsorships.** Local nonprofit sponsorship pages usually link back to sponsors. Choose causes the client genuinely supports.
- **Local press coverage.** Pitch local newspapers, local TV news web pages, regional magazines for feature coverage. Local press tends to keep links live forever.

**Realistic outcome:** A focused local citation effort produces 15-40 placements in the first 90 days, most foundational and one-time. Industry associations and sponsorships compound year over year.

### 3. Partner and Vendor Links

Often overlooked. Existing business relationships are the easiest source of legitimate backlinks.

**Tactics:**

- **Vendor backlinks.** Companies whose products or services the client uses. Many vendors have "customer stories" or "where we work" pages. Cardinal Ridge installs GAF shingles; GAF has a contractor locator that links out. Apply.
- **Partner backlinks.** Companies the client partners with (referral partners, complementary services). Mutual links on partner pages.
- **Client testimonial trade.** Write a genuine testimonial for a B2B vendor in exchange for a link from the vendor's testimonials page. Only do this when the testimonial is real and earned.
- **Supplier or franchise networks.** Some categories have formal contractor / franchise networks that maintain directory pages.

**Realistic outcome:** Easy wins. Most clients have 10-30 untapped partner backlinks available. Pursuing them takes a few hours per month.

### 4. Content-Earned Links

Long-form, link-worthy content that earns links without active outreach. The slowest tactic, the most durable when it works.

**Tactics:**

- **Cornerstone resources.** A definitive guide on a topic in the client's space that becomes the reference. Earns links because other writers cite it instead of writing the same explanation.
- **Original data pieces.** As in §1, but here the publish-and-promote cycle is the primary work.
- **Useful tools.** A simple calculator, template, or checklist embedded on the site. Tools earn outsized backlink volumes because they get cited as resources.
- **Year-in-review or trend reports.** Annual industry summaries that earn citations from any writer covering the topic that year.

**Realistic outcome:** A single great piece can earn 30-200+ links over its lifetime. Most attempts earn far fewer. Plan to publish 4-6 pieces per year aimed at link-earning, expect 1-2 to land.

### 5. Reclamation and Maintenance

Reclaim links that should exist but do not, and protect the links that do.

**Tactics:**

- **Unlinked brand mentions.** Search the client's brand name across the web. Many mentions do not link. Reach out, politely ask for the link to be added. Conversion rate is decent because the writer already mentioned you.
- **Broken-link reclamation.** Inbound links pointing to URLs on the client's site that now 404. Either restore the URL or 301 to a relevant live page so the link equity is recovered. (Covered in `technical-audit` but feeds off-page work.)
- **Lost-link recovery.** When Ahrefs or Semrush shows a previously-existing link is gone, check why. Sometimes the source updated their page; sometimes they linked to a competitor instead. Reach out to confirm and, where appropriate, ask for reinstatement.
- **Competitor broken-link prospecting.** Find pages with broken links pointing to competitor content. Offer your equivalent live resource as a replacement. Slow but legitimate.

**Realistic outcome:** Most established sites have 10-50 reclamation opportunities at any given time. Each one is a few minutes of work and a real (if small) win.

## Tool Adapters

### Ahrefs

- **Site Explorer → Overview** for top-level metrics (referring domains, DR, top pages).
- **Site Explorer → Backlinks** for the link-by-link inventory; filter by Dofollow, Newest, Lost.
- **Site Explorer → Best by Links** for the client's most-linked pages (where existing equity sits).
- **Content Explorer → Broken Backlinks** for reclamation opportunities (broken pages with inbound links).
- **Site Explorer → Anchors** for anchor text distribution.

### Semrush MCP

- `mcp__96c65232-927d-4ecd-8575-0da349b2fb3f__backlink_research` for backlink pulls on the client and competitors.
- Backlink Audit tool for spam-flag review (helpful if the client has historical sketchy links).

### Google Search Console

- **Links → Top linking sites** for the most-trusted view of who links to the client.
- **Links → Top linked pages** for where equity is concentrated.
- **Links → Top linking text** for anchor distribution.
- Free, trustworthy on what Google actually sees, less complete than paid tools.

### Free Fallback

- **Ahrefs Backlink Checker (free tier):** top 100 backlinks per domain.
- **Manual brand search:** quoted brand name on Google, capture mentions, check for missing links.
- **Competitor backlink view:** free Ahrefs tool surfaces top 100 of each competitor; useful for identifying patterns.

## What This Skill Will Not Do

This skill refuses to recommend:

- **Private blog networks (PBNs).** Networks of low-quality sites created to link to clients. Detectable, manual-action risk is real.
- **Paid link networks.** Sites that sell links openly or via brokers. Same risk as PBNs.
- **Guest posting at scale on low-quality sites.** A handful of bylined posts on real publications is fine. A monthly volume of guest posts on third-tier "SEO blogs" is a link scheme. Google's spam team is explicit about this.
- **Reciprocal link schemes.** "Link to us, we link to you" arrangements between unrelated sites for SEO purposes. Different from genuine partner backlinks (where the relationship is real).
- **Comment spam, forum spam, profile spam.** Unambiguous spam.
- **AI-generated outreach at scale.** Mass-personalized AI emails sent to thousands of journalists or site owners. Recipients detect it, sometimes report it to spam services, and the sender's domain gets flagged.
- **Buying expired domains for 301 redirects.** "Link blasting" via expired domain authority. Google has been collapsing this for years.

If the user asks for any of these, decline clearly and explain why. Offer the equivalent white-hat alternative (digital PR instead of paid links, expert sourcing instead of guest posting at scale).

## Common Pitfalls

- **Chasing high-DR sites that are irrelevant.** A backlink from a DR 90 site about pets means nothing for a roofing client. Topical relevance matters more than raw authority.
- **Over-optimizing anchor text.** If 40% of inbound links use the exact target keyword as anchor text, Google's algorithms will flag the profile. Natural distribution skews to brand name, URL, and generic ("click here," "this article") anchors.
- **Ignoring lost links.** Existing referring domains that disappear are a leak in the bucket. Reclaim before chasing new.
- **Treating one viral piece as a strategy.** One link-earning piece is luck. A repeatable publish-and-promote cadence is a strategy.
- **Skipping the on-page check first.** Links to a page that does not match search intent or has weak content will not move rankings. Make sure the destination page deserves the link.
- **Conflating brand mentions with backlinks.** Both have SEO value (especially for AEO), but they are different. Track separately.
