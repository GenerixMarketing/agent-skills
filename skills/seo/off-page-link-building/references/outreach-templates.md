# Outreach Templates

Tested email templates for the most common off-page outreach scenarios. Adapt each to the client's voice (per `client-context.md`) and to the specific recipient. Personalization beats template adherence every time.

Three rules across all templates:

1. **Keep it short.** 100-150 words max. Reporters and editors triage email in seconds.
2. **Lead with what is in it for them.** Their story, their reader, their need. Not the client's wish.
3. **Make the ask explicit.** Vague closings ("looking forward to your thoughts") get no replies. Specific asks ("Would you like a 200-word quote by Thursday?") get responses.

## 1. Expert Sourcing Response (HARO / Connectively / Qwoted / Featured)

Use when responding to a reporter's source request on a sourcing platform.

```
Subject: [Reporter's topic], [Client] expert source

Hi [Reporter name],

Saw your request on [platform] looking for sources on [topic]. I'm [name], [title] at [client], based in [location] and working in [industry] for [N years].

A few quick thoughts you're welcome to use as a source quote:

[Quote 1: a specific, useful insight. 30-50 words. Concrete examples, not generic statements.]

[Quote 2: an angle the reporter might not have considered. 30-50 words.]

Happy to expand on any of this, send photos, or get on a 10-minute call before your deadline if useful.

[Phone number] / [email]

[Signature: name, title, client, link to bio page]
```

Notes:

- Match the reporter's writing style if you can tell from the request.
- Always include phone in addition to email; reporters under deadline call.
- Do not pitch the client's service. The link comes from being a useful source, not from selling.

## 2. Reactive PR Pitch (News Event Hook)

Use when a relevant news event creates a 24-48 hour window to pitch reporters.

```
Subject: [News topic], local expert available

Hi [Reporter name],

With [news event] this week, you might be looking for local context. I'm [name], [title] at [client], with [N years] in [industry] across [region].

Three angles I can speak to:

1. [Specific angle relevant to the event. 1-2 lines.]
2. [Second angle.]
3. [Third angle.]

Available for a call today or tomorrow. I can also share [specific resource: photos, data, customer examples] if useful.

[Phone] / [email]
```

Notes:

- Send within 24 hours of the event for best response rate.
- Identify 3-5 specific reporters covering the beat in your market before the event happens. The fastest pitch wins.
- Local TV news web pages are often higher-value than print: they keep article URLs live forever and earn organic traffic.

## 3. Broken-Link Reclamation Outreach

Use when the client has inbound links pointing to URLs that now 404.

```
Subject: Broken link on [their page title]

Hi [contact name],

I was reading your [page title] and noticed the link to [URL of broken page] returns a 404.

For context, that URL was [original content description]. We've since moved it to [new URL], same content, just a different path.

Would you mind updating the link when you have a moment? Happy to confirm anything you need.

Thanks for the original mention, appreciated then and still does.

[Signature]
```

Notes:

- Be polite about the original link, not entitled.
- Make the fix easy: provide the new URL ready to paste.
- Conversion rate is decent (15-30%) because the writer already chose to link.

## 4. Unlinked Brand Mention Reclamation

Use when a publication mentioned the client by name without linking.

```
Subject: Quick note on your [article title] piece

Hi [writer name],

Reading your piece on [topic], appreciated the mention of [client name].

If it's not too much trouble, would you be open to linking the mention to [URL]? Easier for readers to find the [specific page reason], and we'd be grateful.

Either way, thanks for covering the topic. Looking forward to your next piece on [related topic].

[Signature]
```

Notes:

- Keep the ask casual. The writer chose to mention you; the link is a small additional favor.
- Conversion rate: 20-40% when the publication has editorial flexibility, much lower for major publications with strict link policies.

## 5. Partner / Vendor Backlink Request

Use when a vendor or partner has a logical place on their site to link to the client.

```
Subject: Quick favor / [client] page on your site?

Hi [contact name],

We've been working with [vendor / partner] on [project / volume / years] and I noticed your [partners / customers / installers / contractors] page lists [type of business].

Would it be reasonable to add [client] to that list? Happy to send a short blurb, logo, and link details if useful, should take 5 minutes on your end.

If there's a different page or contact this would be better routed to, just let me know.

[Signature]
```

Notes:

- Reference the relationship specifically (volume, years, project). Generic asks fail.
- Offer to do the work (provide assets).
- Vendor partner pages are one of the easiest backlinks any business can claim and most go unclaimed.

## 6. Local Press Pitching (Feature or Profile)

Use to pitch local newspapers, magazines, or TV news web pages for a feature story.

```
Subject: Story idea, [angle relevant to publication's beat]

Hi [reporter or editor name],

I have a story idea that might fit [section / beat]:

[One-paragraph pitch: the story, why it matters to the publication's readers, what's specific or visual about it. 80-100 words.]

I'm [name], [title] at [client]. We're [credential: years in business, scale, notable customer, awards, etc.]. Happy to provide [interview, photos, customer references, data] for the piece.

If this fits, I can have all of the above ready within [timeline]. If not, I'll keep an eye on the section for the right angle later.

[Phone] / [email]
```

Notes:

- The pitch is about the story, not the client. The client gets credit by being the source.
- Read three recent articles from the reporter before pitching. Reference the beat in your pitch.
- Local press lifecycles: many pitches land months after the first email when the reporter comes back to old idea files. Do not assume silence is a no.

## 7. Podcast Guest Pitch

Use to pitch as a guest on a podcast in the client's space.

```
Subject: Guest idea for [podcast name], [topic angle]

Hi [host name],

Long-time listener, your [recent episode] with [previous guest] on [topic] was excellent.

I'd love to pitch myself as a guest on a related angle: [specific topic, 1-2 sentences on why it's interesting and underdiscussed].

I'm [name], [title] at [client]. Credentials: [N years in industry, specific accomplishments, prior podcast appearances if any]. Happy to send links to past appearances or a sample of my writing on the topic.

If the angle fits, what does your booking process look like?

[Signature, links to bio and any past appearances]
```

Notes:

- Reference a specific past episode. Hosts get pitched constantly by people who clearly have not listened.
- Pitch a specific topic, not "anything you'd find useful."
- Most podcast appearances earn 1 link from the show notes; some earn 5+ if the host is generous with cross-linking.

## 8. Cold Refusal Acknowledgment

Use when you receive a "no" to any of the above. Always reply.

```
Hi [name],

Totally understood, appreciate you taking the time to reply at all.

If anything changes or another angle comes up, you know where to find me. Best with [the piece they're working on / their season / their next project].

[Signature]
```

Notes:

- Build relationships over time. A polite reply to a no often unlocks a yes 6 months later.

## What Not to Send

- **Aggressive follow-ups.** One follow-up after a week is fine; three follow-ups makes you spam.
- **Group templates with the personalization variable still in.** "Hi {FIRST_NAME}" reaching the inbox is a career-ending mistake for any link builder.
- **Insincere flattery.** "I'm a HUGE fan of your work" written to someone you've clearly never read is detectable in two lines.
- **Unsolicited bulk pitches via AI.** As of 2026, several publications and platforms maintain shared blocklists of obvious AI-generated outreach. Use AI to draft and personalize; do not use it to send.
- **"Link in exchange for X" trades.** Any explicit trade is a Google webmaster guidelines violation. Genuine partnerships are fine; explicit trades are not.

## How to Track Outreach

A simple spreadsheet works for most clients:

| Date | Outreach type | Target | Contact | Pitch summary | Response | Outcome | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Track every send. Track every response. Conversion rates inform which workstreams to scale and which to drop.
