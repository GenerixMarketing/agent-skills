# Citation Sources

The foundational citation sources every local business should claim, plus industry-specific directories for major categories. Pulled from a 2026 baseline; verify each is still active before pitching.

NAP consistency (Name, Address, Phone) across every listing matters more than the count. A 20-citation profile with consistent NAP outperforms a 50-citation profile with mismatches.

## Tier 1: Universal Citations (Claim First)

Every local business should have these. Allow 4-6 hours total to claim and complete all of them.

- **Google Business Profile.** The single most important. Categories, services, hours, photos, posts, Q&A, attributes. Treat as ongoing work, not one-time setup.
- **Apple Maps / Apple Business Connect.** Increasingly important for iOS users.
- **Bing Places for Business.** Smaller share, but free and easy.
- **Yelp.** Even if the client does not love Yelp, it ranks. Claim it.
- **Facebook Page (Business).** Counts as a citation; also a social channel.
- **Yellow Pages (YP.com).** Still ranks.
- **Better Business Bureau (BBB).** If applicable in the country. US, Canada, Mexico.
- **Foursquare / Factual data feed.** Provides data to many downstream apps.
- **Nextdoor for Business.** Especially valuable for hyperlocal services.

## Tier 2: Aggregator Data Feeds (One Submission, Many Listings)

These distribute business data to dozens of downstream sites. One submission, many citations.

- **Localeze (Neustar).** Major aggregator. Submitting here populates many smaller directories.
- **Data Axle (formerly Infogroup).** Same. Major data feed for US local data.
- **Acxiom / LiveRamp.** Data infrastructure that powers many local listing sites.

Many SMB clients can pay a tool like Yext, Moz Local, Whitespark, or BrightLocal to manage Tier 1 + Tier 2 in one workflow. Worth it if the client has 10+ locations or struggles with NAP consistency.

## Tier 4: Local-Specific Sources

Vary by metro. For Indianapolis (used as the worked example for Cardinal Ridge):

- Indy Chamber business directory
- Indianapolis Business Journal directory listings
- Visit Indy business listings (if applicable to hospitality)
- Indianapolis Star business directory
- Local neighborhood association websites with member businesses listed

For other metros, identify the equivalents:

- The major regional newspaper's business directory
- The chamber of commerce
- The convention and visitors bureau
- The regional business journal
- Major local lifestyle publications and city magazines
- Neighborhood associations and HOAs that maintain vendor lists

## NAP Consistency Standards

Pick one canonical version of each and use it identically across every citation.

- **Name:** Match the legal business name. Do not append keywords ("Cardinal Ridge Roofing Indianapolis Roof Replacement"). Google penalizes keyword-stuffed business names.
- **Address:** Pick one format. "Street" vs "St", "Suite 100" vs "Ste. 100", "Indianapolis, IN 46220" vs "Indianapolis, IN 46220-1234". Consistency matters more than format.
- **Phone:** One number, formatted identically. (XXX) XXX-XXXX is standard for US; pick one and stick with it.

When the client changes any of the three (rebrands, moves, adds a phone line), update every Tier 1 and Tier 2 listing on the same day. Tools like Yext, Moz Local, and BrightLocal exist for this exact reason.

## Citation Audit Cadence

- **Initial setup:** All Tier 1 within 30 days. Tier 2 + relevant Tier 3 within 90 days.
- **Quarterly:** Audit Tier 1 listings for accuracy. Photos current? Hours updated for holidays? Posts published recently?
- **Annually:** Full audit. Identify any new directories that have emerged in the category.
- **Triggered audit:** Any change to NAP triggers an immediate sweep.

## Citations You Do Not Need

- **General "submit to 500 directories" services.** Most of those directories have zero traffic and zero authority. Cheap to buy, worse than useless because the resulting profile looks campaign-built.
- **Foreign-language directories for English-only businesses.** Stick to the markets the business actually serves.
- **Old aggregators that have lost their feeds.** Some directories from 2010-2015 still exist as zombie sites. Verify the source is still active before submitting.
