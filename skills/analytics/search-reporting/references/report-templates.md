# Report Templates

Starter templates for monthly tactical, quarterly strategic, and annual review search reports. Each template is a starting structure; adapt to the audience and the brand. Every template treats SEO and AEO as co-equal first-class data.

## Monthly Tactical Report

Audience: marketing team, agency-to-client, internal stakeholders.

Length: 4-8 pages or equivalent dashboard view.

Cadence: ships within 5 business days of month-end.

```markdown
# Search Performance Report: [Client], [Month YYYY]

**Reporting period:** [Date range covered]
**Prepared by:** [Person or agency]
**Distribution:** [Stakeholders]

## Executive Summary

[Five sentences or fewer covering: headline number with direction, the most important driver, the most important risk or concern, the most important upcoming work, the headline ask if any.]

## Top-Line Metrics

### SEO Surface

| Metric | This month | Last month | Change | YoY |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Organic sessions | N | N | +/-% | +/-% |
| Organic conversions | N | N | +/-% | +/-% |
| Average position on tracked keywords | N | N | +/- | +/- |
| GSC impressions | N | N | +/-% | +/-% |
| GSC clicks | N | N | +/-% | +/-% |
| Indexation health (indexed URLs / total) | N% | N% | +/- | +/- |

### AEO Surface

| Metric | This month | Last month | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| AI referral sessions (custom channel) | N | N | +/-% |
| Mention rate across ChatGPT/Claude/Perplexity/Gemini | N% | N% | +/- |
| Citation rate across same engines | N% | N% | +/- |
| AI Overview presence on tracked queries | N of N | N of N | +/- |
| Position-in-response distribution | [Brief] | [Brief] | [Direction] |
| Competitive citation share | N% | N% | +/- |

## What Changed

[3-7 specific deltas since last report, each with a 1-2 sentence hypothesis for why. Examples:]

- **Organic traffic to /resources/X grew 23% MoM.** Hypothesis: page-2-push refresh shipped on [date] moved position from #14 to #9.
- **AI citation rate for [query type] dropped from 12% to 4%.** Hypothesis: competitor published a strong piece in [month]; assessing whether to refresh our equivalent.
- **[Specific change with hypothesis.]**

## Work Shipped This Month

| Piece | Type | Cluster | Owner | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|

[Pieces published, pieces refreshed, technical fixes, distribution work, AEO entity work, etc.]

## What Did Not Work

[Honest list of work that did not produce expected results, or expected results that did not appear. 2-4 items.]

- [Specific item with diagnosis or next step]

## Next Period Focus

| Work | Expected outcome | Owner | Timing |
|---|---|---|---|

[3-7 specific items shipping next period, with the outcome each is designed to drive.]

## Notes and Caveats

[Measurement gaps, anomalies that need context, dependencies on other workstreams, anything the audience should know to interpret the numbers correctly.]
```

## Quarterly Strategic Review

Audience: brand owner, marketing executive, board.

Length: 10-20 pages or equivalent presentation.

Cadence: ships within 2 weeks of quarter-end.

```markdown
# Quarterly Search Review: [Client], [Q# YYYY]

**Quarter:** [Q1 / Q2 / Q3 / Q4 YYYY]
**Prepared by:** [Person or agency]
**Audience:** [Specific named stakeholders]

## Quarter at a Glance

[Half-page summary: where the program stands, the biggest win, the biggest concern, the recommended next move. Visual: a single chart showing the quarter's outcome metric trend.]

## Outcomes Against Goals

| Goal | Q-start baseline | Q-end actual | Variance | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Organic-attributed leads | N | N | +/-% vs goal | On track / Behind / Ahead |
| AI citation share in primary cluster | N% | N% | +/- | [Status] |
| [Other quarterly goals] | | | | |

## SEO Surface Performance

### Traffic and Conversions

[Quarterly traffic trend chart, with key inflection points annotated. Quarterly conversion trend chart with same annotation.]

### Ranking Movement

- Keywords gained in top 10: [count, with sample]
- Keywords lost from top 10: [count, with diagnosis]
- Featured snippets gained or lost: [count, with examples]

### Indexation and Technical Health

[Status of any major technical issues, schema deployments, CWV trends. Refer to technical-audit findings if relevant.]

## AEO Surface Performance

### Mention and Citation Rates

[Quarterly trend across ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Gemini. Show both mention rate and citation rate; the gap between them is meaningful.]

### AI Overview Presence

[Quarterly trend on the tracked query set. Specific gains or losses called out.]

### Competitive Citation Map

[Which competitor domains are gaining or losing citation share in the brand's category queries. Identify any pattern shifts.]

### Entity Authority Indicators

- Wikipedia category-article presence change
- Named methodology adoption (third-party citations)
- New backlinks from sources cited in AI engines
- External profile consistency status

## Cluster-Level Performance Review

For each cluster in the active strategy:

### [Cluster Name]

- **Cluster status against plan:** [On track / Behind / Ahead]
- **SEO performance:** [Specific metrics for the cluster]
- **AEO performance:** [Specific metrics for the cluster]
- **Pages shipped this quarter:** [List]
- **Pages refreshed this quarter:** [List]
- **Next-quarter focus:** [Specific work]

## Strategic Recommendations

[3-5 strategic recommendations for the coming quarter. Each one specific and resourced.]

1. **[Recommendation]:** [Rationale]. [Owner and effort estimate]. [Expected outcome.]

## Risks and Dependencies

[Honest list of risks: dependencies on other workstreams, capacity constraints, competitive moves, algorithm risk, market shifts.]

## Investment vs Outcome

[Rough ROI framing: hours invested in the quarter, outcomes attributed. Acknowledged measurement limitations.]

## Notes

[Anything not covered above worth flagging.]
```

Cadence: ships 30-45 days after year-end.

```markdown
# Annual Search Program Review: [Client], [YYYY]

**Year:** [YYYY]
**Prepared by:** [Person or agency]
**Audience:** [Specific named stakeholders]

## Year at a Glance

[One-page summary: the program's biggest accomplishment of the year, the biggest miss, the strategic direction confirmed or pivoted, the budget ask for next year.]

## Year-Over-Year Outcome Comparison

| Outcome metric | YYYY-1 (prior year) | YYYY (this year) | Change | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Organic sessions | N | N | +/-% | |
| Organic-attributed conversions | N | N | +/-% | |
| AI referral sessions | N | N | +/-% | |
| Citation rate across major AI engines | N% | N% | +/- | |
| Pipeline / revenue attributed to search | $N | $N | +/-% | |

## Major Strategic Moves and Their Outcomes

[Each strategic move made during the year, with outcome.]

### [Strategic move]

- **Context:** what drove the decision
- **Investment:** what was spent (hours, dollars)
- **Outcome:** what happened
- **Assessment:** worth it, mixed, did not work

[Honest assessments. The strategic-move review is what separates an annual report from a marketing brochure.]

## SEO Surface: Year in Review

- Traffic trend over the full year
- Conversion trend over the full year

- Top 20 pages by conversion contribution

## AEO Surface: Year in Review

- AI citation trend over the full year
- Mention rate trend

## What Worked

[3-5 specific things that worked, with evidence.]

## Strategic Direction for [YYYY+1]

## Budget Ask for [YYYY+1]

[Reference to the data sources, dashboards, and detailed reports backing the annual review.]
```

## Adapting Templates to Audience

The same data needs different framing per audience.

### For a non-technical brand owner

- Emphasize outcomes (leads, revenue, brand visibility) over channel metrics
- Translate AEO into business language ("how often AI tools mention us when prospects ask")
- Use visual summaries over data tables
- Provide a 2-sentence interpretation for every chart

### For a technical marketing team

- Emphasize the underlying levers (specific keywords, specific pages, specific clusters)
- Provide deeper data access (Looker Studio links, exportable spreadsheets)
- Less narrative interpretation, more raw numbers with context
- Surface anomalies for the team to investigate

### For an executive or board audience

- Emphasize strategic context (competitive positioning, market shifts, channel mix evolution)
- Reduce tactical detail
- Surface investment-vs-outcome framing
- Highlight risks that would change the strategy

### For a budget review

- Lead with ROI framing
- Show the program against the strategic goals it was funded for
- Acknowledge limitations and risks honestly
- Provide the forward-looking ask with clear rationale

A report adapted to its audience gets read and acted on. A report that ignores its audience gets opened once and filed.

## Anti-Patterns Across All Templates

- **Burying the conclusion at the end.** Always lead with the headline.
- **Treating every metric as equally important.** Pick the 5-8 that matter; subordinate the rest.
- **Reporting only what looks good.** Honest reports build trust over time.
- **Forgetting AEO in the SEO report.** In 2026, search reporting that covers only Google is incomplete.
- **Skipping the next-period focus.** A report that does not say what is next leaves the audience without a clear conversation.
- **Inconsistent format between reports.** Audiences develop muscle memory for report structure; changing it every month forces them to re-learn each time.
- **No version control.** Reports get referenced months later; without version control, comparing reports across time becomes guesswork.
