# Platform Distribution by AI Engine

How AI engines pull from different external platforms because of partnership, licensing, or technical relationships. This shapes which off-site platforms matter most for AEO visibility on each engine.

The core insight: each AI service builds models from different data sources, some heavily favored due to commercial agreements. Distributing your content to the platforms each engine favors is one of the cheapest AEO levers most brands ignore.

## The Engine-to-Platform Map

As of 2026. Verify periodically; partnerships change.

### ChatGPT / OpenAI

Microsoft is OpenAI's largest commercial partner, which biases ChatGPT toward Microsoft-owned platforms.

- **LinkedIn** (Microsoft-owned). Highest-value off-site platform for ChatGPT visibility on professional content. Brand presence, founder profiles, employee posts, company-page activity, LinkedIn articles, and LinkedIn Learning content all feed signal.
- **GitHub** (Microsoft-owned). High-value for technical content, developer-facing brands, open-source projects. Documentation, READMEs, and active contributions get cited in ChatGPT responses to technical queries.
- **Reddit**. OpenAI announced a data licensing agreement with Reddit in 2024. Reddit threads are heavily cited in ChatGPT, especially for "best of," "recommendations," and "experience with" queries.
- **Your own site** (via GPTBot direct crawling). Foundation.

Optimization priority for ChatGPT: invest in LinkedIn + GitHub (if technical) + Reddit + own site. Skip TikTok and Instagram unless the brand has unique visual content there.

### Claude / Anthropic

Claude is more reliant on training data than live retrieval for default conversational use.

- **Reddit**. Anthropic also reached a data agreement with Reddit. Claude cites Reddit threads heavily.
- **Your own site** (via ClaudeBot and anthropic-ai). Foundation.
- **Academic sources** (arXiv, university publications, peer-reviewed work). Claude leans on academic sources more than competitor engines, especially for technical or research-adjacent queries.
- **Wikipedia and Wikidata**. Heavily weighted across all major engines but especially in Claude's responses where training-data accuracy matters.

Optimization priority for Claude: invest in own-site authority, Wikipedia category-article presence, Reddit, and (where the brand has research credibility) academic publication or quotation.

### Perplexity

Perplexity is the most live-retrieval-driven engine. Almost every response retrieves the open web at query time and cites sources visibly.

- **Real-time web** via PerplexityBot. Foundation.
- **News sources**. Strong weighting on current news, especially time-sensitive queries.
- **Authoritative editorial publications**. Wirecutter, NYT, major trade press, established industry publications.
- **Reddit** (less than for ChatGPT but still cited).

Optimization priority for Perplexity: SEO-style ranking on the brand's own site (Perplexity's retrieval correlates with traditional rank more than other engines), plus digital PR to get the brand cited in major editorial publications.

### Gemini / Google AI Overviews

Gemini is tightly integrated with Google Search infrastructure.

- **Anything that ranks well in Google Search**. The closest of the four major engines to a "good SEO = good AEO" relationship.
- **YouTube** (Google-owned). High-value for video-relevant queries.
- **Reddit** (because Reddit content ranks well in Google).
- **Wikipedia** (extensively cited in AI Overviews).
- **Your own site** via Google-Extended (training) and traditional Googlebot (live indexing).

Optimization priority for Gemini and AI Overviews: invest in traditional SEO foundations, YouTube presence for visual / instructional categories, and Wikipedia category-article work.

## Cross-Platform Implications

A few patterns that fall out of the engine-by-engine view:

- **Reddit is a top-three priority for three of the four major engines** (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini). For most brands serious about AEO, Reddit presence is non-optional. See `reddit-strategy` for the detailed playbook.
- **LinkedIn is dramatically more valuable for ChatGPT than for other engines.** Brands that have under-invested in LinkedIn often see disproportionate ChatGPT gains when they ramp it up.
- **Wikipedia matters across all four**, but especially Claude and Gemini. See the Wikipedia contribution guide in `entity-presence`.
- **GitHub is engine-specific and category-specific.** Only relevant for technical-content brands and primarily ChatGPT. Most non-technical brands can skip.
- **YouTube is Gemini-specific within the AI engines** but also has standalone SEO value via Google Search. Investing here serves multiple workstreams.

## Distribution Cadence per Platform

Healthy presence levels for AEO purposes (adjust to the brand's bandwidth):

- **LinkedIn (company page):** 2-3 posts per week, plus founder/executive personal posts 1-2x weekly. Articles published quarterly minimum.
- **Reddit:** see `reddit-strategy`. The 90/10 rule applies: 90% helpful contribution, 10% disclosed self-related content.
- **GitHub (technical brands only):** active project work, regular commits to public repos, well-maintained READMEs, documentation kept current.
- **YouTube (visual / instructional brands):** 1-2 substantive videos per month. Quality matters more than volume.
- **Wikipedia:** quarterly review for category-article presence. Edits only when genuinely warranted.
- **Wikidata:** review on rebrand, leadership change, or material company event. Otherwise annual.
- **News and PR:** ongoing via the digital PR workstream in `off-page-link-building`.

## Distribution Anti-Patterns

- **Cross-posting the same content unchanged across every platform.** Detected as low-effort. Adapt the framing per platform.
- **Linking aggressively from social posts back to the brand site.** Most platforms downrank link-heavy content; LinkedIn, Reddit, and YouTube especially.
- **Outsourcing platform-specific content to a generalist agency.** LinkedIn, Reddit, and GitHub each have community norms that require genuine fluency.
- **Treating distribution as a one-time campaign.** It is a sustained presence game. Quarterly platform audits keep the cadence honest.

## How to Use This Reference

When planning the entity-presence workstream:

1. Identify which AI engines matter most for the brand's audience (run a baseline AEO measurement per `citation-tracking` to see where the brand is and is not appearing).
2. Cross-reference against the engine-to-platform map above.
3. Prioritize the 2-3 platforms that serve the engines where the brand most wants to gain visibility AND where the brand has the credibility to participate authentically.
4. Build the distribution cadence into the entity-presence workstream alongside profile consistency and Wikipedia work.

Distribution and entity work compound. A brand with strong consistent profiles AND active presence on the right platforms builds entity authority faster than a brand doing either alone.
