# Before-and-After Examples

Worked rewrites of common page openings. Each shows the same intent reframed for citation. Use as patterns; do not paste verbatim.

## Example 1: Service Page Opening (Local Service Business)

**Target query:** "roof replacement indianapolis"

**Before:**

> Welcome to Cardinal Ridge Roofing, your trusted Indianapolis roofing partner. We've been serving homeowners across the metro area with quality roof replacement and repair services since 2003. Our experienced crews take pride in delivering exceptional craftsmanship on every project, big or small. Whether you're dealing with storm damage, a leak, or simply an aging roof, we're here to help you protect your home and family.

What is wrong: throat-clearing opening, no specific claim, no extractable definition or statistic. AI engines reading the first 200 words find nothing to cite.

**After:**

> A residential roof replacement in the Indianapolis metro typically takes one to three days and costs $9,000 to $25,000, depending on the size of the roof and the materials chosen. Cardinal Ridge Roofing has completed over 2,000 of these replacements across Marion, Hamilton, Hendricks, and Johnson counties since 2003. About 60% of our jobs are insurance claims following storm damage; 40% are planned replacements on roofs 15+ years old. We handle the insurance paperwork in-house, work primarily with GAF and CertainTeed asphalt systems, and offer a 5-year workmanship warranty in addition to the manufacturer's material warranty.

What changed: specific timeline (1-3 days), specific price range, named geography, specific volume (2,000+ jobs), specific date (2003), specific split (60/40), named materials (GAF, CertainTeed), specific warranty (5-year workmanship). Every sentence is independently citable.

## Example 2: Cost Guide Page Opening

**Target query:** "how much does a roof replacement cost in indianapolis"

**Before:**

> Roof replacement is a significant investment for any homeowner. The cost can vary widely based on many factors, and it's important to understand what goes into pricing before getting quotes. In this guide, we'll explore the various elements that affect roof replacement costs in the Indianapolis area.

What is wrong: announces what it will do instead of doing it. Vague phrasing. No specific number for an AI engine to extract for a "cost" query.

**After:**

> A standard asphalt shingle roof replacement on a 2,000-square-foot Indianapolis home costs between $9,000 and $14,000 in 2026. Metal roofing on the same home runs $18,000 to $30,000. Three factors drive most of the cost variance: roof complexity (multiple peaks, valleys, and dormers add 15-30%), tear-off depth (one layer of existing shingles is standard; two layers add $1,500-3,000), and material choice (architectural shingles average $1.50 more per square foot than 3-tab). Below: the full breakdown by material, roof size, and project type.

What changed: leads with specific numbers, names the year for freshness, breaks down cost drivers concretely, signals what is in the body of the page. AI engines extracting for the cost query find the answer in sentence one.

## Example 3: "What Is" Definition Page

**Target query:** "what is answer engine optimization"

**Before:**

> The world of search marketing is constantly evolving. As artificial intelligence continues to reshape how users find information online, marketers and SEO professionals are facing entirely new challenges. One approach that has emerged in response to these changes is something called Answer Engine Optimization, or AEO for short. In this comprehensive guide, we'll dive deep into what AEO means, why it matters, and how to get started.

What is wrong: 70 words before defining the term the page is supposed to define. Anyone landing on this page asking "what is AEO" has to scroll.

**After:**

> Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) is the discipline of getting your content surfaced and cited by AI answer engines like ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Gemini, and Google AI Overviews. It overlaps with traditional SEO but differs in three ways: the win condition is citation inside an AI response (not ranking in a list), the unit of competition is a quotable passage (not a page), and the measurement set is multi-platform (not just Google). Brands invest in AEO because AI engines now intermediate a growing share of search queries, and being cited is the new analog to ranking.

What changed: definition in the first sentence, comparison to a familiar concept (SEO) in the second, business context in the third. A complete answer to "what is AEO" in 90 words.

## Example 4: Comparison Page Opening

**Target query:** "asphalt shingle vs metal roof"

**Before:**

> When it's time to choose a roofing material for your home, you have many options. Two of the most popular are asphalt shingles and metal roofing. Each has its own advantages and disadvantages that should be considered based on your specific situation, budget, and aesthetic preferences. Let's take a closer look at how they compare.

What is wrong: announces what it will do, frames as "it depends," delays the comparison.

**After:**

> Asphalt shingles cost less upfront ($9,000-14,000 for a typical Indianapolis home) and last 20-30 years. Metal roofing costs more upfront ($18,000-30,000) and lasts 40-70 years. For homeowners planning to stay 10 years or less, asphalt is usually the better economic choice. For homeowners staying 15+ years or buying for resale value, metal often comes out ahead. Below: the full comparison across cost, lifespan, energy efficiency, hail resistance, weight, and insurance impact.

What changed: side-by-side specific numbers in the first three sentences, decision framework in the fourth, page outline in the fifth. A reader looking for the answer to the comparison gets it; AI engines extracting for the comparison get a clean side-by-side.

## Example 5: How-To Page Opening

**Target query:** "how to file a roof insurance claim"

**Before:**

> Dealing with a roof insurance claim can be an overwhelming experience for any homeowner. The process involves several steps and can take weeks or even months to complete. Understanding what to do (and what not to do) can make a big difference in the outcome of your claim. This guide walks you through the process from start to finish.

What is wrong: empathy throat-clearing, no specific step, no extractable how-to content.

**After:**

> A roof damage insurance claim in Indiana typically takes 30 to 90 days from the initial inspection to the final payout. The seven steps are: (1) document the damage with timestamped photos, (2) call your insurance company within the policy's reporting window (usually 30 days), (3) request a claims adjuster visit, (4) get an independent contractor inspection in parallel, (5) review the adjuster's scope of work line by line, (6) negotiate any disputed items in writing, (7) schedule the work after claim approval and payout. Most homeowners win 80-100% of their claim when they follow this sequence; the most common reason for partial payouts is failing to document pre-existing condition before the loss.

What changed: specific timeline, numbered explicit steps, success-rate statistic, named common-failure mode. Every step is extractable; the success rate is citable; the failure mode is a quotable contrarian insight.

## Patterns That Apply Across All Examples

A few patterns repeat across the rewrites:

- **The first sentence answers the implicit question of the page.** Always.
- **Specific numbers and named entities appear before vague generalizations.** Always.
- **The page is laid out so a skim of the opening reveals the structure of the full answer.** Always.
- **Throat-clearing phrases ("welcome to," "in today's," "have you ever wondered," "let's explore") are absent.** Always.
- **Subordinate clauses framing the topic come after the topic, not before.** ("Cardinal Ridge has completed 2,000+ replacements since 2003" beats "Since 2003, Cardinal Ridge has been completing 2,000+ replacements.")

When in doubt, write the first paragraph last, after you have written the rest of the page, then check that the first paragraph stands alone as a complete answer. If it does not, rewrite.
