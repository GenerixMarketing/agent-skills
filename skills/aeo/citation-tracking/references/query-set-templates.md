# Query Set Templates

Starter query sets for common business categories. Adapt the brackets and the geography for the specific client. Aim for 20-30 queries total across the five categories (problem-aware, category recommendation, comparison, expert recommendation, brand-direct).

These templates are starting points. The query set should evolve over the first 2-3 months as you learn which queries actually surface meaningful patterns for the specific brand.

## Template 1: Local Service Business (e.g., Roofing, Plumbing, Dental)

Replace `[service]` and `[city]` throughout.

**Problem-aware (5):**

- I have a leak in my [service-relevant problem], what should I do
- How do I know if I need a new [service]
- What is the first sign of [service-relevant problem]
- My [thing] is [N] years old, do I need to replace it
- Storm damage to my [thing], who do I call

**Category recommendation (6):**

- Best [service provider] in [city]
- Top rated [service provider] in [city]
- Affordable [service provider] in [city]
- [service provider] near [neighborhood within city]
- Family-owned [service provider] in [city]
- [service provider] who handles insurance claims in [city]

**Comparison (4):**

- [Material A] vs [Material B] for [use case]
- Should I repair or replace my [thing]
- DIY vs hiring a professional for [service]
- [Brand of material A] vs [Brand of material B] [type of product]

**Expert recommendation (4):**

- Who should I hire for [service] in [city]
- Recommendations for [service] in [city]
- What [service] company has the best reviews in [city]
- Reliable [service] contractor [city]

**Brand-direct (5):**

- Describe [brand name] in one paragraph
- Is [brand name] a good [service provider]
- [Brand name] reviews
- How long has [brand name] been in business
- What does [brand name] specialize in

## Building Your Own Query Set

If none of the templates match the client's category exactly:

1. Start with the five categories: problem-aware, category recommendation, comparison, expert recommendation, brand-direct.
2. Aim for 4-6 queries per category.
3. Write each query as a real human would type or speak it. Test by reading it aloud; if it sounds like a marketing person wrote it, rewrite.
4. Pull at least 5 queries directly from sources of real customer language: support tickets, sales call transcripts, the client's ICP language section in client-context.md, top PAA results for the category, top Reddit threads in relevant subreddits.
5. Include at least one geo-modified query if location matters to the brand's business.
6. Include at least one "is [brand] worth it" or "describe [brand]" query for entity-direct testing.

Once the set is built, lock it. Re-evaluate quarterly, not monthly.
