# Intake Questions

The full question bank for the `client-context` skill. Walk through these in order, one section at a time. Do not ask all of them at once. Let the user skip questions they cannot answer and mark those as `TBD`.

Each section has required questions (ask every time) and optional questions (ask when the client is more mature or the project warrants it).

## 1. Business Basics

**Required:**

1. What is the brand or company name?
2. What is the primary website URL? (Use the canonical version with or without trailing slash, whichever the client uses.)
3. In one or two sentences, what does the business sell or do?
4. Is the business B2B, B2C, or both?
5. Where do they operate? (City, region, country, or fully online?)

**Optional:**

6. How long has the business been operating?
7. What stage is the business in? (Startup, growth, established, mature)
8. Are there other brands, sub-brands, or sister sites worth knowing about?
9. Is the business venture-backed, bootstrapped, family-owned, franchised, or something else?

## 2. Ideal Customer Profile

**Required:**

1. Who is the primary customer? (Demographics, role, life stage, company size, etc.)
2. What problem are they trying to solve when they find this business?
3. What are the top three things this customer cares about when making a buying decision?
4. What are the top three objections or hesitations that stop them from buying?

**Optional:**

5. Are there secondary customer segments worth noting? (e.g., a SaaS that sells to both individual users and enterprise teams)
6. Where does this customer spend time online? (Reddit subs, YouTube channels, podcasts, communities)
7. What does the customer search for when they are at the top of the funnel? Mid-funnel? Bottom?
8. What does the customer call themselves and the problem they have? (Their words, not the industry's words.)

## 3. Competitors

**Required:**

1. Name the top three direct competitors (companies selling the same thing to the same audience).
2. Are there any aspirational competitors? (Brands the client wants to be compared to, even if not direct rivals.)
3. When the customer searches for the client's main keywords, what other types of results show up? (Reddit threads, YouTube videos, listicles, marketplaces, review sites.)

**Optional:**

4. Are there competitors that have grown quickly in the last 12 months? Why?
5. Are there competitors the client deliberately wants to differentiate from?
6. Are there any competitors who have lost ground recently? (Useful for spotting opportunity.)
7. Has the client ever lost a deal to a competitor? Why?

## 4. Voice and Brand

**Required:**

1. Pick three to five adjectives that describe how the brand should sound.
2. Are there industry terms or internal jargon the client wants kept in?
3. Are there words, phrases, or styles the client wants kept out?
4. Is there an existing style guide or example content I should match?

**Optional:**

5. Does the brand use em dashes? Oxford comma? Sentence-case or title-case headings?
6. What pronouns and point of view does the brand use? (We/our/us? Direct second-person? Third-person?)
7. Are there founder or executive voices worth matching?
8. Are there regulated terms to avoid? (Common in finance, health, legal, real estate.)

## 5. Technical Setup

**Required:**

1. What CMS does the site run on? (WordPress, Webflow, Shopify, Wix, Squarespace, custom, etc.)
2. Is Google Search Console verified for the domain? Can the client share access?
3. Is Google Analytics (GA4) installed? Can the client share property access or a service account?

**Optional:**

4. What hosting platform is the site on?
5. What other SEO or analytics tools does the client use? (Ahrefs, SEMRush, Screaming Frog, Moz, Plausible, Fathom, etc.)
6. Are there any AI citation or AEO tracking tools in place? (Brand Radar, Profound, Otterly, etc.)
7. Does the site have a CDN? Cloudflare, Vercel, Netlify?
8. Are there active dev resources, or is content the only lever?
9. Are there any pending migrations, redesigns, or replatforms in the next six months?

## 6. Goals and Constraints

**Required:**

1. What is the primary goal of this engagement? (Lead gen, ecommerce sales, brand awareness, AI citations, organic traffic growth, ranking for specific terms, etc.)
2. How will the client know it worked? What metric defines success?
3. What is the timeline? (Quarter, year, no fixed deadline)
4. Are there things off the table? (No design changes, no dev work, no content on certain topics, legal restrictions)

**Optional:**

5. What is the budget range, if relevant?
6. Who at the client side will review and approve work?
7. Are there past SEO efforts that worked or failed? What was the takeaway?
8. Has the site been hit by an algorithm update or manual action? When?
9. Is there an internal team that handles execution, or is the client looking for the agent to handle implementation too?

## Notes on Asking

- Lead with the URL question. Visiting the site early lets you answer some questions yourself before asking the user.
- For voice and competitor questions, ask the client to share examples or links instead of just describing in words. "Send me three URLs you like" beats "tell me how you want to sound."
- For ICP questions, push past demographics. "Mid-market SaaS marketers" is not enough. "Marketing managers at 50-500 person B2B SaaS companies who own SEO and report to a VP of marketing" is enough.
- For competitor questions, always ask about shadow competitors. Reddit, YouTube, and review sites take more traffic in AEO contexts than direct rivals do.
- If the user gets fatigued, stop and write what you have. Mark the rest as `TBD`. You can fill in more during the next session.
