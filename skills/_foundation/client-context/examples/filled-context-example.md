# Client Context: Cardinal Ridge Roofing Co.

> This is a fictional example for illustration. Brand name, URL, competitors, and all specifics are made up. Any resemblance to a real company is coincidental.

**Last updated:** 2026-05-15
**Updated by:** Jon Eric

## Business Basics

- **Brand:** Cardinal Ridge Roofing Co.
- **URL:** https://www.example.com/
- **What they sell:** Residential roof replacement and repair, with a smaller commercial flat-roof line. Insurance claim work is a meaningful portion of the residential business.
- **Where they operate:** Greater Indianapolis metro area (Marion, Hamilton, Hendricks, and Johnson counties). In-person service only.
- **Business stage:** Established. Family-owned, second generation, in business since 2003.

## Ideal Customer Profile

- **Primary audience:** Homeowners aged 35-65 in the Indianapolis metro who have a damaged or aging roof. About 60% come in after a storm event (hail, wind), 40% come in for planned replacement on a roof that is 15+ years old.
- **What the audience cares about:**
  1. Whether the company will handle the insurance claim paperwork
  2. Whether the crew shows up when they say they will
  3. Whether the work will actually last (warranty length, materials used)
- **What stops the audience from buying:**
  1. Fear of fly-by-night storm chasers (a real issue in this market)
  2. Sticker shock on the quote
  3. Uncertainty about whether insurance will actually cover the work
- **Secondary audiences:** Property managers handling small multi-family buildings (4-20 units) and a small number of HOAs.

## Competitors

- **Direct competitors:**
  - Competitor A (large local roofer with heavy radio advertising)
  - Competitor B (mid-sized roofer known for fast insurance turnaround)
  - Competitor C (newer entrant aggressively targeting suburban neighborhoods)
- **Aspirational competitors:** A large regional player with strong brand presence and a polished site
- **Shadow competitors:**
  - Reddit threads in r/Indianapolis asking for roofer recommendations
  - YouTube reviews of roofing material brands (GAF, CertainTeed, Owens Corning)
  - Angi and HomeAdvisor listing pages that rank for "Indianapolis roofer" terms
  - Insurance company referral lists (major carriers push customers toward preferred contractors)

## Voice and Brand

- **How they sound:** Trustworthy, plainspoken, locally rooted, no-nonsense, warm
- **Words they use:** "Crew" (not "team"), "roof system" (not "roofing solution"), "ma'am" and "sir" when speaking to customers, "neighbor" when referring to local homeowners
- **Words they avoid:** "Storm chasers" (industry term that has negative connotations they do not want to invoke), aggressive sales language ("act now," "limited time"), corporate jargon
- **Style notes:** Oxford comma yes. No em dashes. Sentence-case headings. Refer to the business as "Cardinal Ridge" on second mention, not "we" or "our team" alone.

## Technical Setup

- **CMS:** WordPress (Divi theme, owner is hands-off and relies on a freelance web person for changes)
- **Hosting:** SiteGround
- **Analytics:** GA4 installed. Property ID available. Some legacy UA tags still firing.
- **Search Console:** Verified. Owner has access. Service account available on request.
- **Other tools the client uses:** None currently. No Ahrefs, no SEMRush. Open to adding a tool if it justifies cost.

## Goals and Constraints

- **Primary goal:** Increase qualified inbound leads from organic search and Google Business Profile. Specifically: more "roof replacement [neighborhood]" type queries converting into estimate requests.
- **Success metric:** Estimate-request form submissions per month from organic traffic, plus phone calls tagged as organic source via call tracking. Baseline: 22 estimates per month from organic. Target: 50 per month within 9 months.
- **Constraints:**
  - No major site redesign in the next six months (budget is committed elsewhere)
  - Cannot publish content that suggests insurance fraud or aggressive claim tactics (legal sensitivity)
  - Owner reviews and approves all blog posts personally, expects 48-72 hour turnaround on review
  - Cannot promote specific roofing material brands in a way that suggests exclusivity (they install multiple brands)
- **Timeline:** 12-month engagement, quarterly check-ins
- **Budget range:** Mid-range for an SMB engagement, room to grow if results show
